Description

Good old Southern mashed potatoes are the ultimate comfort foodâ€”creamy, buttery, and made with love. Boiled until perfectly tender, the potatoes are mashed smooth with generous helpings of real butter, a splash of whole milk, and just the right amount of salt and pepper. Sometimes a touch of sour cream or a hint of garlic is added for extra richness, but the heart of this classic dish lies in its simplicity and warmth. Served hot and fluffy, they're a staple at Sunday dinners, holiday feasts, and family gatherings all across the South.

Ingredients

  • 2-3 lbs small red potatoes

  • 3/4 cup milk

  • 1/4 cup chicken broth (or enough to cover the potatoes)

  • 4 tablespoon of butter

  • salt, pepper, and peprika

steps

  1. Scrub the potatoes and if you prefer peeled potatoes peel them now

  2. Dice the potatoes so that they are small and close in size (dicing them to similar sizes will help ensure the potatoes cook evenly)

  3. Season the potatoes with salt,pepper and peprika then cover with chicken broth and bring to a boil

  4. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 20 minutes or until fork tender

  5. In a small sauce pan melt the butter and then stir in milk until combined

  6. Pour mixture over potatoes and mash until desired consistency
